Bypassing Cloudflare WAF in Bug Bounty
Programs

1. Introduction: Understanding Cloudflare WAF and the Importance of
Bypasses in Bug Bounty Programs


Web application security is a fundamental pillar in the contemporary digital landscape. With
the increasing complexity of cyberattacks, Web Application Firewalls (WAFs) have become an
indispensable first line of defense. Cloudflare WAF stands out as one of the most prominent of
these solutions, offering comprehensive protection for millions of websites and applications
worldwide. However, the effectiveness of these solutions does not mean they are entirely
impenetrable; attackers, especially Bug Bounty Hunters, constantly seek ways to bypass these
defenses.
Overview of Cloudflare WAF and its Role in Web Application Protection
Cloudflare WAF acts as a vital shield positioned between a web application and the Internet.
Its primary mission is to inspect incoming web and API requests and filter out unwanted traffic
based on sets of rules known as "rulesets".1 This firewall provides immediate protection for
web applications by analyzing incoming traffic and determining whether it is malicious or
not.3Cloudflare WAF is an integral part of Cloudflare's vast global network, which is capable of
processing immense volumes of HTTP requests, reaching up to 106 million requests per
second during peak times.3 This global architecture enables Cloudflare to leverage global
threat intelligence, which is continuously refined by analyzing two trillion requests daily,
providing unparalleled protection against the latest attacks, including zero-day exploits and
emerging threats.3Cloudflare WAF's main functions include "Detection" of malicious activities
and "Mitigation" of them. In terms of detection, the WAF identifies malicious or potentially
malicious activities through advanced mechanisms such as "WAF Attack Score," which
evaluates traffic based on its likelihood of being malicious, and "Bot Score," which determines
whether traffic originates from a bot or a human.2 As for mitigation, the WAF blocks suspicious
requests, challenges them (e.g., by displaying a CAPTCHA test), or throttles them via features
like custom rules, managed rules, and rate limiting rules.2Cloudflare WAF aims to protect
applications from a wide range of common attacks, including OWASP Top 10 threats like SQL
injection and Cross-Site Scripting (XSS), in addition to preventing credential stuffing attempts
by detecting leaked credentials, and identifying malware in uploaded files.3 It also offers
unmetered DDoS Protection, ensuring the continuous availability of applications.3
The Importance of Discovering WAF Bypasses in the Context of Bug Bounty Programs
Despite the critical role of Web Application Firewalls (WAFs) as security gateways that inspect
incoming HTTP traffic to filter out malicious requests and provide defenses against a variety
of web-based threats, they are not immune to evasion.10 Evading WAFs can compromise these
defenses, potentially causing significant harm to Internet users.10A successful WAF bypass in
bug bounty programs is considered a critical security finding because it allows malicious
payloads to reach the underlying web application undetected or unblocked, which can lead to
severe consequences.11 These consequences include data breaches, unauthorized system
access, Denial of Service (DoS) attacks, website defacement, and malware injection.11WAF
bypasses are often highly valued in bug bounty programs, as they expose fundamental
weaknesses in the application that the WAF was supposed to protect.13 Several published Bug
Bounty reports highlight Cloudflare WAF bypasses as serious security issues, especially when
the original server IP address is exposed.13 However, bug bounty program policies vary in this
regard; while some programs (like Tesla's program) may consider WAF bypasses out of scope
if they do not lead to direct exploitation 17, other programs (like Imperva's program) explicitly
encourage WAF bypass testing.14 This variation underscores the importance of understanding
each program's requirements to determine what constitutes a "reportable WAF bypass."
Why WAFs Remain Vulnerable to Bypass Despite Their Evolution
Despite continuous advancements in WAF technologies, they remain vulnerable to bypass for
several fundamental reasons. Many WAFs, especially those relying on a Negative Security
Model, depend on signatures to block known threats.7 This makes them ineffective against
zero-day attacks and novel payloads that do not match existing signatures.7 Attackers
constantly develop new evasion techniques, making it a continuous arms race.12Limitations in
request inspection contribute to vulnerabilities that attackers can exploit. For example, some
WAFs may only inspect a portion of the bytes in an HTTP request, allowing attackers to pad
their payloads with additional data to bypass inspection.18 Many WAFs also struggle to inspect
encrypted traffic (e.g., HTTPS) without enabling deep packet inspection.18 Additionally, WAFs
often lack the necessary context to understand how data is used by the application, making
them susceptible to attacks that exploit logical flaws within the application
itself.11Misconfigurations and logical gaps in WAF policies are significant factors contributing
to vulnerabilities.12 Incorrectly configured rules or exceptions can inadvertently open
pathways for attackers. The need to balance security with user experience often leads to less
stringent rules to minimize false positives, creating opportunities for bypass.20One of the
more sophisticated reasons for successful bypasses is the exploitation of parsing
discrepancies between the WAF and the web application.10 The WAF might interpret an HTTP
request differently from how the backend application interprets it, allowing a malicious
payload to pass through the WAF's filters but be correctly parsed and executed by the
application. This approach is more complex than simple obfuscation techniques.

2. Cloudflare WAF Mechanism and Advanced Features


Cloudflare WAF operates as an integral part of the Cloudflare global network, offering a wide
array of features designed to protect web applications and APIs from evolving threats. The
WAF's operation is best understood by examining its different rule types and the advanced
detection mechanisms it employs.
Managed Rules and Custom Rules
Cloudflare WAF relies on robust rulesets to filter traffic and determine if it is malicious. These
rules are primarily divided into two types:
   ●​ Managed Rules:
         ○​ These are pre-configured rules created by the Cloudflare security team, providing
             immediate and effective protection against known security vulnerabilities, such as
             those listed in the OWASP Top 10, as well as the ability to counter zero-day
             exploits.2
         ○​ These rules are regularly updated to ensure coverage of new vulnerabilities and
             reduce false positives, guaranteeing continuous protection with minimal
             disruption.1
         ○​ These sets include the Cloudflare Managed Ruleset, the Cloudflare OWASP Core
             Ruleset (Cloudflare's implementation of the OWASP ModSecurity Core Rule Set),
             Cloudflare Exposed Credentials Check rules that verify credentials against public
             leaked databases, and Cloudflare Sensitive Data Detection rules that monitor for
             personal or financial information leakage.23
         ○​ Users can modify the behavior of specific rules within these sets or create
             exceptions to handle false positives, providing configuration flexibility.1
   ●​ Custom Rules:
         ○​ These rules give users precise control over incoming traffic by filtering requests
             based on specific expressions using Cloudflare's Rules language.1
         ○​ Custom rules can take various actions when a request matches the defined
             criteria, such as "Block" for malicious requests, "Managed Challenge" to present
             interactive tests (like CAPTCHA), "JS Challenge," "Log" to monitor traffic without
             blocking it, or "Skip" to dynamically disable Cloudflare security features for a
             specific request.4
         ○​ Custom rules are evaluated in a specific order, and some actions like "Block" stop
             the evaluation of other rules, affecting the request processing flow.2
         ○​ The ability to create custom rules and support for Regular Expressions (Regex)
             varies based on the Cloudflare plan (Free, Pro, Business, Enterprise), with
             higher-tier plans offering more flexibility and capabilities.24
   ●​ Rate Limiting Rules:
         ○​ These rules are used to define limits for the rate of incoming requests that match
             a specific expression, and to specify the action to take when these limits are
             reached. This feature is crucial in stopping Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks and
             Brute-force attempts targeting applications and APIs.1
Advanced Detection Mechanisms
In addition to rules, Cloudflare WAF uses advanced detection mechanisms to identify threats:
   ●​ Attack Score:
         ○​ This feature evaluates traffic on a scale from 1 (likely to be malicious) to 99
             (unlikely to be malicious). It checks for known attack variations and malicious
             payloads.1
         ○​ This system is machine learning-based, trained on real positive samples observed
             across Cloudflare's vast network, allowing it to detect new evasion, bypass, and
             attack techniques even before they become publicly known.5
   ●​ Bot Score:
         ○​ This feature evaluates traffic on a scale from 1 (likely to be a bot) to 99 (likely to be
             human).1 Cloudflare relies on a variety of signals, including data collected from
             Project Honey Pot, to improve the accuracy of bot detection.27
   ●​ Leaked Credentials:
         ○​ This feature scans incoming requests for credentials (usernames and passwords)
             previously leaked from known data breaches, helping to prevent account takeover
             attempts.1
   ●​ Malicious Uploads:
         ○​ This feature inspects content objects, such as uploaded files, for malicious
              signatures like malware, protecting servers from malware infection.1
   ●​ Firewall for AI Beta:
         ○​ A new feature that helps protect services powered by Large Language Models
              (LLMs) from abuse and AI-specific attacks.1
These detection mechanisms enrich incoming requests with rich metadata, which is available
in the "Security Analytics" and "Security Events" dashboards. Users can analyze this data to
better understand their security posture and determine the most appropriate mitigation rules.1
Cloudflare WAF Architecture and its Role as a Reverse Proxy
Cloudflare WAF operates on the Cloudflare global network, sitting in front of web applications
as a Reverse Proxy.3 This means it intercepts all incoming requests before they reach the
Origin Server, inspecting them against its rule engine and global threat intelligence. After
inspection, legitimate requests are routed to their intended destination, while suspicious
requests are blocked, challenged, or logged.3This architecture offers several benefits,
including leveraging global threat intelligence gathered from millions of websites, machine
learning-based detection that addresses emerging threats in real-time, and rapid deployment
and easy management without the need to deploy any on-premise hardware or software.3

Section 2 Insights: In-depth Analysis
  1.​ Cloudflare's Multi-layered Defense and Potential Bypass Vectors: Cloudflare WAF
      does not represent a single, monolithic defense; rather, it is a combination of
      signature-based managed rules that are frequently updated 2, user-defined custom
      rules that offer significant flexibility 24, and advanced detection mechanisms such as
      ML-powered attack scores 2 and bot scores.27 The rule execution order 2 and the "Skip"
      action 25 are crucial to how requests are processed. This layering, while increasing
      defensive strength, also introduces multiple potential points of failure or bypass. A
      bypass may not necessarily defeat all layers but might slip through a specific rule type
      or a misconfigured "Skip" action. For instance, a sophisticated attack might bypass a
      signature-based managed rule but be detected by the ML model (attack score), or vice
      versa if the ML model is not perfectly tuned. Therefore, bug bounty hunters should
      adopt a multi-layered testing approach, attempting to bypass specific rule types or
      detection mechanisms. For example, if a simple XSS payload is blocked by managed
      rules, obfuscation should be attempted. If that fails, it should be checked whether the
      WAF's ML model (attack score) was triggered. Misconfigurations in "Skip" actions
      should also be sought, as an administrator might have inadvertently created an
      exception that allows malicious traffic.
  2.​ Limitations of "Human Speed" in WAF Defense: While Cloudflare prides itself on
      rapid rule activation and machine learning to address unknown vulnerabilities 4, one
      source acknowledges that "human speed becomes a limiting factor" in updating
      Cloudflare's managed rules.5 This implies that whenever a new attack vector is
      discovered, a "window of opportunity" becomes available for attackers to bypass
      mitigations before rules are fully updated. The Log4j RCE attack is cited as an example
      where frequent rule updates were necessary due to the discovery of new bypasses
      resulting from changes in known attack patterns.5 This is a critical observation for bug
      bounty hunters. It means that newly discovered vulnerabilities (especially Zero-days or
      N-days) are prime targets for WAF bypasses, as WAF rules may not have fully adapted
      to all bypass variations. Researchers should closely monitor vulnerability disclosures
      and test for bypasses quickly before WAF vendors can fully update their rulesets.
      Although the ML-powered attack scoring system is designed to mitigate this, it is not
      foolproof, as evidenced by the need for continuous rule updates for Log4j.
3. Common Cloudflare WAF Bypass Techniques


Bypassing Web Application Firewalls (WAFs) is an art that requires a deep understanding of
how these defenses work and their potential weaknesses. Despite the evolution of Cloudflare
WAF, there are several techniques that attackers and bug bounty hunters can use to bypass it.
Payload Encoding & Obfuscation
      These techniques aim to alter the representation of the attack payload to make it harder
      for the WAF to detect malicious patterns, while ensuring the payload remains
      executable by the web application.8 Common techniques include:
  ●​ Hex encoding: Replacing characters with their hexadecimal equivalent. For example,
      SELECT can be encoded as %53%45%4C%45%43%54.8 If the WAF does not correctly
      decode the payload, it might miss the attack.
  ●​ Base64 encoding: Converting the entire payload into a Base64 string.8
  ●​ URL encoding: Encoding certain characters as %xx, where xx is the hexadecimal
      representation of the character.8 Double encoding (e.g., using %25 instead of %) can
      bypass poorly designed filters that lack recursion in the decoding process.28
  ●​ Unicode encoding: Using Unicode alternatives for ASCII characters (e.g., \u0070 for p
      in prompt()).28 This can trick WAFs that do not fully process Unicode characters.
  ●​ HTML encoding: Encoding special characters into HTML entities (e.g., " for ").28
  ●​ Case Transformation: Changing the case of characters in the payload (e.g., SeLeCt
      instead of SELECT) to bypass case-sensitive pattern matching in less sophisticated
      WAFs.8
  ●​ Using Comments: Inserting comments (/**/, /*!--*/) into attack strings to break patterns
      that the WAF looks for.28 In databases like MySQL, the /*! */ syntax can be used for
      conditional comments that are executed by the database but might be ignored by the
      WAF.28
  ●​ Junk Characters/Padding: Inserting random or meaningless characters (+-+-1-+-+) or
      padding payloads with extra characters. This technique exploits the fact that some
      WAFs may only inspect a portion of the bytes in a request.18
  ●​ Line Breaks/Newlines/Tabs: Inserting line breaks (%0A) or tabs (%09) to obfuscate
      payloads and break patterns that the WAF looks for.28
  ●​ Encrypted Payloads: WAFs often struggle to inspect encrypted traffic (e.g.,
      HTTPS/TLS) without enabling deep packet inspection, allowing attacks to slip through
      encrypted communication channels.18
Parsing Discrepancies
      These techniques are more sophisticated, exploiting inconsistencies in how WAFs and
      web application frameworks interpret HTTP requests. This allows malicious payloads to
      pass through the WAF but be correctly parsed and executed by the application.10
  ●​ HTTP Parameter Pollution (HPP): Involves sending multiple parameters with the same
      name in a single request. The WAF might only inspect the first parameter, while the
      server processes a subsequent parameter value containing the malicious payload.8
  ●​ HTTP Parameter Fragmentation (HPF): Splitting the malicious payload across multiple
     parameters, making it difficult for the WAF to reassemble and detect the full payload.30
  ●​ JSON and XML Payloads: Many modern web applications use REST APIs and SOAP
     services that transmit data in JSON or XML formats. Historically, many WAFs, including
     Cloudflare, did not fully or correctly inspect JSON or XML content, allowing malicious
     code injection within these formats.8 Although Cloudflare has recently added JSON
     support, these vulnerabilities were common.33
  ●​ Content-Type Manipulation: Changing or removing the Content-Type header to trick
     WAFs into not decoding/evaluating requests based on known content types. For
     example, a WAF might not inspect the payload if the content type is unexpected.34
     Research shows bypasses across Cloudflare for multipart, json, xml content types.10
  ●​ Null Byte Injection (%00 or 0x00): Exploits how different programming languages
     and systems handle null bytes, causing premature string termination and bypassing
     input validation checks.35 This technique can be used to manipulate file paths and
     bypass file extension checks in file upload scenarios.35
Exploiting Logical Flaws and WAF Misconfigurations
     WAFs primarily analyze request content and lack the full context of application logic,
     making them vulnerable to attacks that exploit business logic flaws.11 Misconfigurations
     are also a common cause of bypasses, even in advanced WAF systems.12
  ●​ Session Awareness Bypassing: Exploits stateless WAFs that process each request
     independently. Attackers can spread an attack across multiple requests, where
     innocuous payloads are sent first, followed by the malicious payload that exploits a
     vulnerability not detected in the context of a single request.8
  ●​ Exploiting Application Logic Flaws: WAFs may not understand how data is processed
     during deserialization, allowing attacks to slip through.19 This includes issues like Broken
     Access Control where attackers can exploit weaknesses in application logic to bypass
     access restrictions.18
  ●​ Misconfigured Rules/Exceptions: Administrators can inadvertently create exceptions
     or rules that allow malicious traffic. For example, a "Skip" rule in Cloudflare WAF might
     be configured too loosely, unintentionally disabling security features for certain
     requests.25
  ●​ Zero-Day Exploits: WAFs are ineffective against new and unknown vulnerabilities until
     their signatures are added to the WAF database.7 Although Cloudflare uses machine
     learning to address this challenge, it is a continuous race between attackers and
     defenders.5
Direct Access to Origin Server (Origin IP Exposure)
     Cloudflare acts as a reverse proxy, meaning it hides the Origin Server's IP address
     behind its network. If the original IP address is exposed, attackers can bypass
     Cloudflare WAF entirely by sending requests directly to the server, thereby
     circumventing all layers of protection provided by Cloudflare.8
  ●​ Methods for Origin IP Detection:
        ○​ Historical DNS Records: Analyzing old DNS records for the site, which might
            reveal the original IP address before the site was placed behind Cloudflare.27
         ○​ Email Headers: Extracting server IP addresses from email headers sent by the
            application.27
         ○​ Content/Favicon Search: Searching for unique content or favicons associated
            with the original IP address.37
         ○​ Misconfigured DNS/Subdomains: If CNAME records are not configured
            correctly, or if subdomains point directly to the origin server instead of
            Cloudflare.15
         ○​ Server Information Leakage: Error messages, misconfigured services, or
            internal network leaks might reveal the original IP address.
   ●​ Impact: This bypass allows attackers to send unfiltered payloads directly to the server,
      bypassing all common Cloudflare protections, enabling them to perform Denial of
      Service (DoS) attacks and retrieve sensitive data.13
Header Manipulation
Header manipulation involves modifying HTTP headers to trick the WAF or application into
processing requests differently or bypassing security checks.
   ●​ X-Originating-IP, X-Forwarded-For, X-Remote-IP, X-Remote-Addr Headers: Setting
      these headers to 127.0.0.1 (localhost) or other internal IP addresses can bypass WAFs
      configured to trust themselves or upstream proxies.34 This exploits excessive trust in
      headers that can be forged.
   ●​ Content-Type Header: Changing or removing this header. Some WAFs may only
      decode or evaluate requests based on known content types, allowing bypass if an
      unexpected content type is used.34 Research shows bypasses across Cloudflare for
      multipart, json, xml content types.10
   ●​ Host Header: Modifying the Host header to bypass poorly configured WAFs that rely on
      the correct Fully Qualified Domain Name (FQDN) of the site.34
   ●​ x-middleware-subrequest Header: A critical vulnerability in Next.js applications
      (CVE-2025-29927) revealed that manipulating this header could bypass authentication,
      authorization, and rate limiting systems.38 While not a direct WAF bypass, it highlights
      how header manipulation can bypass security controls behind the WAF. WAFs can be
      configured to block this header.38
   ●​ User-Agent/Referer Headers: Spoofing the User-Agent header to mimic legitimate
      browsers or the Referer header to appear as if the request is coming from a trusted
      source, to bypass bot detection or filtering rules based on these headers.27
IP Fragmentation and Other Techniques
   ●​ IP Fragmentation: Involves splitting the payload into multiple smaller IP fragments to
      bypass WAFs that fail to reassemble packets completely before inspection.8
   ●​ H2c Smuggling: A lesser-known technique capable of bypassing WAF restrictions.21
   ●​ Large Request Bodies: WAFs typically ignore data exceeding a configurable maximum
      body size. This can be exploited for POST, PUT, and PATCH requests. For example,
      Cloudflare Enterprise has a 128 KB body size limit for managed rulesets.21

Section 3 Insights: In-depth Analysis
  1.​ The Vulnerability of "Normalization" and "Parsing Discrepancies": Available
      resources frequently highlight that "parsing discrepancies" and "vulnerabilities in WAF
      request normalization functions" are among the most significant causes of bypasses.10
      The core idea here is that the WAF and the backend application might interpret the
      same HTTP request differently after normalization or parsing. For example, the WAF
      might remove certain characters or decode part of the payload in one way, while the
      application decodes or processes it differently, allowing a malicious payload to pass
      through the WAF's filters but be correctly processed by the application. This approach
      is more complex than simple obfuscation, as it aims not just to hide the payload but to
      exploit the difference in "understanding" the request between the WAF and the
      application. JSON/XML payloads 8 and content-type manipulation 34 are specific
      examples of this type of bypass. Therefore, bug bounty hunters should focus on how
      WAFs normalize, decode, and parse incoming requests compared to how the target
      application does so. This means experimenting with different Content-Type headers,
      various encodings (especially nested or mixed ones), and parameters (HTTP Parameter
      Pollution, HTTP Parameter Fragmentation) to identify where this discrepancy lies.
      Specialized tools like the "Bypass WAF" Burp Suite extension 34 and SQLMap's tamper
      scripts 30 are designed to exploit these nuances.
  2.​ Misconfiguration as a Key Bypass Vector, Even for Advanced WAFs: Although
      Cloudflare WAF boasts advanced features like an ML-powered attack scoring system 2,
      many resources point to "misconfigured policies" 12 and "poorly configured WAFs" 34 as
      major enablers of bypasses. The example of origin IP exposure 13 is a direct result of
      misconfiguration (e.g., not properly hiding the origin IP). The "Skip" action in Cloudflare
      WAF 25 also presents a misconfiguration risk if not used carefully, as an administrator
      could inadvertently create an exception that allows malicious traffic. This suggests that
      even the most sophisticated WAF can be rendered ineffective by human error in
      configuration. Therefore, bug bounty hunters should not assume perfect WAF
      configuration. They should actively look for configuration weaknesses, such as exposed
      origin IPs, overly permissive "Skip" rules, or WAFs that trust certain headers (like​
      X-Forwarded-For) without proper validation. This often involves reconnaissance beyond
      just the web application, looking into DNS records, email headers, and network
      infrastructure.

4. Practical Aspects and Tools Used in WAF Bypass


Bypassing Cloudflare WAF in bug bounty programs requires a combination of deep technical
knowledge, creativity, and the use of appropriate tools. Tools are an integral part of the
penetration testing process, enabling researchers to automate complex tasks, experiment
with multiple bypass techniques, and efficiently analyze responses.
Penetration Testing Tools
A suite of tools is essential for any researcher aiming to bypass WAFs:
  ●​ Burp Suite: Considered "the world's #1 web penetration testing toolkit."34 It is an
      indispensable tool for intercepting, inspecting, modifying, and replaying HTTP requests.
         ○​ Burp Intruder: This component is used for launching custom fuzzing attacks and
             high-speed payload delivery, allowing for experimentation with a large number of
             payload variations to discover vulnerabilities.8
         ○​ BApp Store Extensions: These extensions offer powerful additional
             functionalities. Specifically, the "Bypass WAF" extension 8 adds headers like
             X-Originating-IP, X-Forwarded-For, X-Remote-IP, X-Remote-Addr to exploit trust
             configurations in WAFs or backend applications. It also enables modification of
             Content-Type and Host headers, and provides options for path and parameter
             obfuscation, directly targeting WAF weaknesses related to parsing and
             configuration.34
         ○​ Other Extensions: Extensions like Turbo Intruder are used for high-speed
             fuzzing, increasing the efficiency of bypass discovery.8
  ●​ SQLMap: A powerful open-source tool used for automating the detection and
      exploitation of SQL injection vulnerabilities.8
         ○​ Tamper Scripts: These scripts are SQLMap's key feature in the context of WAF
             bypass. They modify payloads (e.g., encoding, case transformation, HTTP
             parameter pollution, fragmentation) to evade WAF rules.8 Examples include
             space2morehash.py, charencode.py, chardoubleencode.py, and
             charunicodeencode.py, which exploit various ways to obfuscate or change
             payload representation to bypass filters.40
  ●​ OWASP ZAP (Zed Attack Proxy): A free, open-source penetration testing tool
      specifically designed for web applications.41
         ○​ It can be configured to automatically test various payloads and attack vectors,
             and supports custom scripting for WAF bypass scenarios.8
         ○​ It is effective for fuzzing and evaluating WAF responses to unexpected inputs,
             helping to identify weaknesses in the inspection process.18
  ●​ Nmap: A versatile network scanner with powerful Nmap Scripting Engine (NSE) scripts.
         ○​ http-waf-detect.nse: This script attempts to determine if a web server is
             protected by a WAF by probing the server with malicious payloads and detecting
             changes in the response code or body.44
         ○​ http-waf-fingerprint.nse: This script attempts to identify the type and version of a
             WAF by sending a number of requests and looking for known behaviors and
             fingerprints in the responses.45
        ○​ Nmap can also be used in IP fragmentation scenarios.8
Fuzzing Techniques for Discovering New Bypasses
Fuzzing is an effective technique for discovering new WAF bypasses. It involves sending
unexpected, malformed, or invalid inputs to the application to evaluate the WAF's response.18
Advanced fuzzing techniques, especially those targeting "parsing discrepancies" in headers
and body segments (e.g., JSON, multipart/form-data, XML), have led to the discovery of
numerous bypasses across various WAFs, including Cloudflare.10 Tools like Burp Intruder,
OWASP ZAP, and Metasploit enable efficient automated fuzzing operations.8
Using Headless Browsers and CAPTCHA Solutions
  ●​ Headless Browsers (e.g., Selenium): These browsers are used to automate browser
      interactions without a graphical user interface. They can simulate human browsing
      behavior (e.g., scrolling, mouse movements, clicks) to trick Cloudflare's bot detection
      systems.27 They can also bypass canvas fingerprinting techniques used in bot
      detection.39
  ●​ CAPTCHA Solutions: Cloudflare uses CAPTCHA challenges as a mitigation measure
      when it suspects traffic is robotic.25 Dedicated CAPTCHA solver services or tools can
      automate the process of solving these challenges, allowing the attack to continue.27 It's
      worth noting that Cloudflare has removed support for older Privacy Pass v1 tokens 46
      and now relies on a variety of signals to infer if incoming traffic is automated.46
The Importance of Proxies and IP Rotation
  ●​ Proxy Solutions: The use of proxies, especially Residential Proxies, is effective in
      evading Cloudflare's bot detection. These proxies provide real IP addresses from
      different geographical locations, making traffic appear legitimate and originating from
      real users.27
  ●​ IP Address Rotation: This technique helps combat IP address blocking and avoid
      suspicion by frequently changing the source IP address for each request or set of
      requests, making it difficult for the WAF to track malicious activities.27
  ●​ Bright Data's Web Unlocker: This is an AI-powered solution offered by Bright Data,
      which uses advanced techniques like browser fingerprinting, CAPTCHA solving, IP
      rotations, and request retries to bypass anti-bot detections like Cloudflare with high
      success rates of up to 99.99%.39
Section 4 Insights: In-depth Analysis
  1.​ Automation and Integration of Techniques for Effective Bypass: Available resources
      emphasize that successful WAF bypass often requires not just one technique, but a
      combination of multiple techniques.8 Tools like Burp Suite, SQLMap, and OWASP ZAP
      provide powerful features for encoding, fragmentation, header manipulation, and
      fuzzing.8 The emphasis on "combining multiple techniques" 8 and "fortified headless
      browsers" with "IP rotation" and "CAPTCHA solving" 39 indicates that a multi-faceted
      and automated approach is often necessary to penetrate advanced WAFs. Therefore,
      bug bounty hunters should move beyond single-technique manual testing. They need to
      master a suite of tools and understand how to chain techniques (e.g., encoding + HTTP
      parameter pollution + header manipulation) to craft complex payloads that exploit
      multiple WAF weaknesses simultaneously. Automation frameworks and custom scripting
      are becoming increasingly important for effectiveness and efficiency in this domain.
  2.​ The Arms Race in WAF Defense and Bypass: Cloudflare continuously updates its bot
      detection algorithms, which are now AI-powered 27, and its managed rulesets are
      regularly updated to address unknown vulnerabilities.23 However, bypass tools and
      techniques are also evolving, with new weaknesses being discovered constantly.39
      Cloudflare's mention of removing support for older Privacy Pass v1 tokens 46
      demonstrates its continuous adaptation to bypass attempts. This is a clear "arms race"
      where both sides are constantly innovating, meaning what works today might not work
      tomorrow. Therefore, bug bounty hunters must stay updated on the latest WAF
      technologies and bypass methods. Relying on outdated techniques will not be effective.
      Continuous learning, monitoring security research (such as the paper on parsing

      in the bug bounty game. This also implies that WAF bypasses are not "find-and-forget"
      vulnerabilities; a bypass discovered today might be patched tomorrow, requiring
      continuous vigilance.

5. Impact of Cloudflare WAF Bypass in Bug Bounty Programs


Bypassing Cloudflare WAF is a discovery of paramount importance in bug bounty programs,
not only because it exposes a weakness in a critical security layer but also because it often
enables the exploitation of deeper vulnerabilities in the underlying application. Understanding
the security risks and consequences of such a bypass, and how to assess its severity and
report it effectively, is essential for successful bug bounty hunters.
Security Risks and Consequences of WAF Bypass
When a WAF is successfully bypassed, applications become exposed to numerous attacks
that the firewall was supposed to prevent. The main security consequences include:
  ●​ Data Breaches: This is one of the most serious outcomes, where attackers can gain
      unauthorized access to and exfiltrate sensitive data, such as personal information,
      credit card numbers, passwords, and confidential business data. This leads to
      significant financial losses, reputational damage, and legal liabilities under data
      protection regulations like GDPR and HIPAA.11
  ●​ Unauthorized Access and System Compromise: By bypassing the WAF, attackers can
      gain access to restricted areas of the web application or underlying systems, such as
      administrative panels, databases, and APIs. They can then modify content, inject
      malicious scripts, create backdoors for future access, or escalate privileges to gain
      greater control over the system.11
  ●​ Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Attacks: With the
      WAF bypassed, attackers can more easily launch DoS or DDoS attacks, overwhelming
      the web application with massive traffic and making it unavailable to legitimate users.
      This can disrupt business operations, lead to revenue loss, and damage customer
      trust.11 WAF bypass can enable attackers to circumvent rate limits enforced by the
      WAF.12
  ●​ Website Defacement: An attacker might deface the website by altering its visual
      appearance or content, often for political, vandalistic, or to undermine trust in the
      targeted organization. Defacement can significantly harm an organization's image and
      requires time and resources to restore the original content.11
  ●​ Malicious Code Injection: Attackers can exploit a WAF bypass to inject malicious code,
      such as Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) payloads or malware, into the web application. This
      can lead to additional attacks against application users, such as cookie theft, session
      hijacking, or delivering malware to users' devices.11
  ●​ Session Hijacking: Attackers can steal or reuse authentication tokens by exploiting
      WAFs that do not enforce strict session expiration or token validation policies.10
  ●​ Escalation of Privileges: Attackers can leverage this vulnerability to escalate their
      privileges, gaining higher-level access to the system.35
  ●​ Impact on Large Language Models (LLMs): If the WAF protects services relying on
      LLMs, WAF bypasses can lead to attacks like prompt injection, training data poisoning,
      or Model DoS.32
How to Assess the Severity of Bypasses and Submit Effective Reports in Bug Bounty
Programs
To increase the chances of a WAF bypass report being accepted and rewarded, bug bounty
hunters should focus on several key aspects:
  ●​ Focus on Practical Exploitation: Many bug bounty programs, including Cloudflare's
      own, emphasize that WAF bypasses alone may not be sufficient; they must demonstrate
      "practical exploitation" or "significant security impact."47 A WAF bypass is often a means
      to an end (exploiting an underlying vulnerability), not the vulnerability itself.22
  ●​ Defense-in-Depth Context: The WAF is part of a multi-layered defense strategy.12
      Bypassing the WAF exposes a weakness in one layer, but the real impact comes from
      exploiting a vulnerability that the WAF was supposed to protect.
  ●​ Clear Proof of Concept (PoC): Reports must include a clear and detailed Proof of
      Concept that demonstrates step-by-step how the WAF was bypassed and the resulting
      impact (e.g., successful SQL injection after WAF bypass, or XSS payload execution). The
      PoC must be reproducible.
  ●​ Severity Assessment: The severity of a WAF bypass depends on the underlying
      vulnerability it enables. A bypass leading to Remote Code Execution (RCE) is considered
      critical, while a bypass leading to minor information leakage might be low/medium
      severity.
  ●​ Detailed Technical Report: The report should provide a detailed technical description
      of the bypass technique, an explanation of why it succeeded against Cloudflare WAF
      (e.g., a specific rule bypassed, or a parsing discrepancy exploited), and the resulting
      impact on the application.

      requirements.

6. Recommendations: Strengthening Defenses Against WAF Bypasses


Cloudflare WAF is a powerful tool in the cybersecurity arsenal, but it is not a silver bullet. To
strengthen defenses against WAF bypasses, organizations and bug bounty hunters must
adopt a multi-faceted approach that combines proper configuration, continuous monitoring,
and integrated security practices.
For Organizations: Best Practices for Cloudflare WAF Configuration
To ensure maximum protection and minimize bypass opportunities, organizations should
follow these practices:
   ●​ Defense-in-Depth Strategy: WAFs should not be relied upon as the sole defense layer;
      they are an additional layer of protection.7 WAFs should be integrated with other
      security layers such as secure coding practices, patch management, server hardening,
      and API gateways.12
   ●​ Proper WAF Configuration and Fine-Tuning:
         ○​ Enable deep packet inspection for all HTTP requests to counter obfuscated or
             encrypted payloads.18
         ○​ Customize rules to detect and block obfuscated payloads.18
         ○​ Utilize anomaly detection systems (like Cloudflare's attack score) that examine
             request behavior instead of solely relying on signatures.5
         ○​ Carefully configure "Skip" actions to avoid unintended bypasses.25
         ○​ Limit the number of rules to the absolute minimum required to avoid latency
             issues and false positives.7
   ●​ Origin IP Protection: Ensure that the origin server's IP address is not exposed to the
      Internet. This is crucial to prevent direct access bypasses.37 A "strict" setup should be
      implemented where the origin server only accepts connections from Cloudflare's IP
      addresses.
   ●​ Regular Monitoring and Logging: Regularly review WAF logs for anomalous requests
      (e.g., unusually large requests, large login payloads, or repeated requests with encoded
      inputs).18 Cloudflare provides "Security Analytics" and "Security Events" tools for this
      purpose.1 Decrypted matched payloads should be stored in logs for deeper analysis.1
   ●​ Continuous Testing: Conduct regular penetration tests and use fuzzing tools to
      evaluate the WAF's response to unexpected inputs.11 This helps identify
      misconfigurations and bypass opportunities.
   ●​ Secure Development Lifecycle (SDL): Address underlying application vulnerabilities
      rather than merely hiding them with a WAF.7 Implement secure coding practices (e.g.,
      using prepared statements, input sanitization, whitelisting, and proper escaping).12
  ●​ Stay Informed: Keep WAF policies and threat intelligence updated.12 Leverage
      Cloudflare's regularly updated managed rulesets.3
For Bug Hunters: Tips for Increasing Bypass Discovery Chances and Submitting
High-Quality Reports
To maximize success in bug bounty programs in the context of WAF bypass, security
researchers are advised to:
  ●​ Deep Understanding of WAFs: Understand how WAFs work, their limitations, and
      common bypass techniques.12
  ●​ Tool Proficiency: Master the use of penetration testing tools like Burp Suite (with its
      extensions), SQLMap (with tamper scripts), and OWASP ZAP.8
  ●​ Think Beyond Signatures: Focus on parsing discrepancies, logical flaws, and
      misconfigurations rather than just simple obfuscation.10
  ●​ Chain Attacks: Always try to chain a WAF bypass to a demonstrable impact on the
      application (e.g., SQL injection, XSS, RCE, data leakage).22
  ●​ Reconnaissance: Investigate for origin IP exposure.13
  ●​ Experiment with Encodings and Headers: Test different encoding schemes, nested
      encodings, and header manipulations.8
  ●​ Fuzzing: Use automated fuzzing to discover unexpected behaviors and bypasses.10
  ●​ Read Program Rules: Understand the specific scope and requirements for WAF
      bypasses in each bug bounty program.14
  ●​ Clear PoC and Impact: Provide a detailed and reproducible Proof of Concept, clearly
      demonstrating the security impact.47
Section 6 Insights: In-depth Analysis
  1.​ The Critical Link Between Secure Coding and WAF Effectiveness: While WAFs are
      an important defensive layer, available resources consistently warn against using them
      as a "quick fix" or to "hide critical vulnerabilities."7 The fundamental problem remains
      unresolved if underlying vulnerabilities are not addressed through secure coding
      practices.12 If developers are not educated about security, they will continue to produce
      insecure code, leading to an endless cycle of WAF rule additions and bypass attempts.7
      Therefore, organizations need to invest in a comprehensive security program that
      prioritizes secure development practices (shifting security left in the development
      cycle). WAFs should complement core application security, not replace it. For bug
      bounty hunters, this means that even if a WAF is present, the underlying application
      vulnerabilities are still the primary targets, and bypassing the WAF is key to proving their
      exploitability.
  2.​ The Evolving Nature of WAFs and the Need for Continuous Adaptation: Cloudflare
      WAF is constantly evolving, incorporating machine learning 3, updating managed rules
      23
        , and deprecating older features like Firewall Rules in favor of WAF Custom Rules with
      new actions like "Skip."25 This continuous evolution means that both defenders and
      attackers must constantly adapt. What works today might not work tomorrow.
      Therefore, for organizations, this means continuous monitoring, rule tuning, and staying
      updated on Cloudflare's recommendations and feature changes. For bug bounty
      hunters, this means the "WAF bypass game" is dynamic. They need to continuously
      research new techniques, understand Cloudflare's latest features and how they can be
      bypassed, and be prepared for rapid changes in detection capabilities. This emphasizes
      the importance of the "continuous testing" recommendation.

7. Conclusion
Cloudflare WAF is a vital line of defense in protecting modern web applications from a wide
range of cyber threats. However, as this report has demonstrated, its effectiveness does not
mean it is entirely impenetrable. WAFs remain vulnerable to bypass due to factors such as
inherent limitations in request parsing, human misconfiguration, and the continuous arms race
between evolving attack techniques and defensive updates.
Bug bounty programs provide a crucial platform for discovering these complex vulnerabilities,
where skilled security researchers can uncover ways to bypass Cloudflare WAF, enabling them
to exploit deeper vulnerabilities in the underlying application. The value of a report in these
programs lies not only in demonstrating the bypass itself but in the ability to link it to a
tangible and reproducible security impact.
To strengthen defenses against WAF bypasses, organizations must adopt a multi-layered
defense strategy that does not solely rely on WAF but complements it with secure
development practices, robust origin IP protection, continuous monitoring, and regular
penetration testing. Conversely, bug bounty hunters must continuously evolve their skills,
master advanced tools, think beyond traditional signatures, and focus on parsing
discrepancies, logical flaws, and misconfigurations to increase their chances of discovering
high-impact bypasses.
